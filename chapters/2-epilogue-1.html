EPILOGUE 1

The dim glow of enchanted lanterns flickered across the dusty shelves of Armaan Singh’s library, casting long shadows over ancient tomes and relics. The old man, in his late fifties, bore every wrinkle and grey streak that told a story of time.

A sharp ring echoed through the house...

Armaan: “Who are you? State your business, lad.”

The young man tilted his head, a faint, knowing smile playing on his lips. He had long golden hair that caught the evening light.

Armaan: “Hold on... I think I’ve seen you before. Those features — they scream of my old comrade-in-arms, Aditya Sharma.”
Abhijeet: “You are sharper than you look, Chacha Armaan. I am the son of your old friend Aditya Sharma. My name is Abhijeet Sharma.”

Armaan froze. “Aditya’s son... Oh my God, you are alive!”

Abhijeet: “When Dad and Mom disappeared, Bhua ji took me to a different country. Now I need to know the truth about The Day Hell Opened.”
Armaan: “I will tell you about it, but it is not a pleasant story... Let’s start. It was the year 2098, Earth Venta.”
